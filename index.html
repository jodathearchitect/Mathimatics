📐 Math Reference Guide
General Math
Order of Operations
PEMDAS — Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division (left→right), Addition/Subtraction (left→right). Always solve what's in parentheses first.
Percentage
Part ÷ Whole × 100 = %. To find part: Whole × (% ÷ 100). Example: 15 of 60 = 15÷60×100 = 25%.
Ratio
a : b means for every a of one thing, there are b of another. To simplify, divide both sides by their GCD.
Proportion
a/b = c/d → cross-multiply: a×d = b×c. Solve for the unknown.
Absolute Value
|x| = distance from zero. Always positive. |−5| = 5. |5| = 5.
Algebra
Solving Equations
Do the same operation to both sides. Isolate the variable. Check your answer by plugging it back in.
Quadratic Formula
x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a for ax²+bx+c=0. The discriminant (b²−4ac) tells you: positive=2 real roots, zero=1 root, negative=no real roots.
Slope-Intercept
y = mx + b. m = slope (rise/run). b = y-intercept (where line crosses y-axis). Slope = (y₂−y₁) / (x₂−x₁).
FOIL (binomials)
(a+b)(c+d) = ac + ad + bc + bd. First, Outside, Inside, Last. Example: (x+2)(x+3) = x²+3x+2x+6 = x²+5x+6.
Exponent Rules
xᵃ × xᵇ = x^(a+b) | xᵃ ÷ xᵇ = x^(a−b) | (xᵃ)ᵇ = x^(ab) | x⁰ = 1 | x⁻¹ = 1/x.
Probability
Basic Probability
P(event) = Favorable outcomes ÷ Total outcomes. Always between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain).
AND Rule
P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) — only if events are independent (one doesn't affect the other).
OR Rule
P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B). For mutually exclusive events (can't both happen): P(A) + P(B).
Complement Rule
P(not A) = 1 − P(A). If P(rain) = 0.3, then P(no rain) = 0.7. They always add to 1.
Permutations
Order matters. nPr = n! / (n−r)!. "How many ways can 3 people finish a race from 10?"
Combinations
Order doesn't matter. nCr = n! / (r! × (n−r)!). "How many 3-person teams from 10?"
Statistics
Mean (Average)
Sum of all values ÷ count of values. Example: (4+7+9+10) ÷ 4 = 30 ÷ 4 = 7.5.
Median
Middle value when sorted. If even count, average the two middle values. Resistant to outliers.
Mode
Most frequently occurring value. A dataset can have no mode, one mode, or multiple modes.
Range
Max − Min. Measures the spread of data. Example: {2, 5, 9, 14} → Range = 14 − 2 = 12.
Standard Deviation
Measures how spread out values are from the mean. Low SD = data clustered near mean. High SD = widely spread.
Variance
Standard Deviation squared (σ²). Average of squared differences from the mean.
Financial Mathematics
Simple Interest
I = P × r × t. Principal × rate × time. Example: $1000 at 5% for 3 years = 1000 × 0.05 × 3 = $150.
Compound Interest
A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt). P=principal, r=annual rate, n=compounds per year, t=years.
Present Value
PV = FV / (1 + r)^t. What is a future amount worth today? Discounting future cash flows back to now.
Future Value
FV = PV × (1 + r)^t. What will an amount be worth in the future given a growth rate?
ROI
Return on Investment = (Gain − Cost) / Cost × 100%. Example: Spent $200, earned $300 → ROI = 100/200 × 100 = 50%.
Break-Even Point
Break-Even = Fixed Costs ÷ (Price per unit − Variable cost per unit). Where revenue equals total costs.
Risk & Probability Models
Expected Value
E(X) = Σ[x × P(x)]. Multiply each outcome by its probability, then add. Example: 50% chance of $100 and 50% chance of $0 → E = 0.5×100 + 0.5×0 = $50.
Risk = Impact × Probability
Risk Score = Severity of harm × Likelihood of occurrence. Used in risk matrices to prioritize threats.
Bayes' Theorem
P(A|B) = P(B|A)×P(A) / P(B). Updates the probability of A given new evidence B. Foundation of conditional probability.
Normal Distribution
68% of data within 1 SD, 95% within 2 SD, 99.7% within 3 SD of the mean. The "bell curve" rule.
Z-Score
Z = (X − μ) / σ. How many standard deviations a value is from the mean. Z=0 is the mean, Z=1 is 1 SD above.

📐 Algebra Terms
Variable
A letter (like x or y) that represents an unknown number. Your goal in algebra is usually to find the value of the variable.
Coefficient
The number multiplied by a variable. In 3x, the coefficient is 3. In −7y², the coefficient is −7.
Expression vs. Equation
Expression: a math phrase with no equals sign (3x + 2). Equation: has an equals sign and can be solved (3x + 2 = 11).
Factor
Numbers or expressions you multiply together. Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. Factoring an equation = splitting it into multiplied parts.
Inequality
A statement that one value is greater or less than another. x > 5 means x can be any number larger than 5. Note: flip the sign when multiplying/dividing by a negative.
🎲 Probability Terms
Sample Space
All possible outcomes of an experiment. Rolling a die: sample space = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}. Probability is calculated from this set.
Independent Events
Two events where one outcome doesn't affect the other. Example: flipping a coin twice — the first flip doesn't change the second.
Mutually Exclusive
Events that cannot both happen at the same time. You can't roll a 2 AND a 5 on the same die roll. P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B).
Conditional Probability
P(A | B) = "probability of A given B has already happened." The vertical bar | means "given." Example: P(rain | cloudy) = probability it rains, given it's already cloudy.
📊 Statistics Terms
Outlier
A data point that is much higher or lower than the rest. Outliers drag the mean away from the center but don't affect the median — that's why the median is called "resistant."
Skewed Distribution
Skewed right (positive): tail stretches right, mean > median. Skewed left (negative): tail stretches left, mean < median. Normal (symmetric): mean = median = mode.
Correlation vs. Causation
Correlation means two variables tend to change together. Causation means one causes the other. Important: correlation does NOT prove causation.
Confidence Interval
A range of values that likely contains the true population parameter. A 95% CI means: if you repeated the study 100 times, about 95 of the intervals would contain the true value.
💰 Financial Terms
Principal
The original amount of money before interest. In $1000 at 5% interest, the principal is $1000. Interest is always calculated on the principal.
Compounding
Earning interest on both the principal AND previously earned interest. "Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world" — the more frequently it compounds, the faster money grows.
Discount Rate
The rate used to calculate present value. Represents the time value of money — a dollar today is worth more than a dollar in the future because today's dollar can grow.
Annuity
A series of equal payments made at regular intervals. Examples: monthly rent, loan payments, pension distributions. Can be valued using present value formulas.
⚠️ Risk Modeling Terms
Expected Value (EV)
The average outcome if you repeated an event many times. Not what happens each time — it's the long-run average. EV of a fair coin flip for $1 = $0.50.
Variance & Standard Deviation
Variance measures how spread out outcomes are. Standard deviation (SD) is its square root — easier to interpret because it's in the same units as the data. High SD = high uncertainty = more risk.
Risk-Neutral vs. Risk-Averse
Risk-neutral: cares only about EV. Risk-averse: prefers a certain smaller gain over an uncertain larger one. Most people are risk-averse — that's why insurance exists.
Monte Carlo Simulation
Running thousands of random simulations to model the probability of different outcomes. Used in finance and engineering to estimate risk when math alone gets too complex.
